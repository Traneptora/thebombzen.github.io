Electromagnetic Field Hockey

Because Oracle now requires that a trusted source sign all Java applets and WebStart applications (which costs hundreds of dollars), I will only provide this program as a downloadable jarfile.
Download Electromagnetic Field Hockey

This is a preliminary version (version dev7), so it will probably contain bugs. Electromagnetic Field Hockey is named after Electric Field Hockey on The University of Colorado's PhET website, but allows multiple moving charges and includes a constant into-the-screen magnetic field. It uses a Runge-Kutta method to track moving charges across time.

Electromagnetic Field Hockey is in the public domain. You can do whatever you want with it as long as you don't break any laws in your jurisdiction.

Electromagnetic Field Hockey is open-source. Get the source on GitHub

Controls:
  • Left click: Place positive charge
  • Right click: Place negative charge
  • Middle click: Remove charge
  • Click and drag: Place charge in motion
  • Boxes in the upper-left: Click the boxes in the upper-left to change between placing moving or stationary charges.
  • Green X: Toggle a uniform into-the-screen magnetic field.
Graphics key:
  • Red particle: Positive charge
  • Blue particle: Negative charge
  • Black circle: Charge is stationary
  • Orange arrows: Electric Field
  • Green Xs: Magnetic Field
